# The Ledger — the epistemic labels, in Euler's own dialect

Every load-bearing claim in a Project EULER session wears exactly one label. The labels are not
decoration — they are the plugin's core discipline, and they are historically Euler's own: he
ran a two-tier epistemic economy in *print*, for decades, and it is why his wrong conjectures
never cost the method anything.

## The labels

- **Demonstrated** — a proof that survives audit (lagrange's counter), or — engineering
  register — two *independent* witnesses (a second run of the same path is one witness with an
  echo). Upgrades to Demonstrated are loud, named events.

- **Observed** — directly computed or witnessed; the case count and exact output stated; the
  instrument calibrated (an uncalibrated reading is UNVERIFIED wearing a lab coat).

- **Conjectured** — the induced law, wearing its evidence openly: which cases *fitted* it
  (spent), which *fresh* cases passed, which rival laws were considered, what boundary is
  untested. Euler's dialect for the strong form: "**a known truth, but not yet demonstrated**"
  (E175, about the pentagonal identity — which he then held at that label for ~ten years while
  using it). His inheritance rule, also from E175: "all the conclusions which may be deduced
  from it will be of the same nature, that is, true but not demonstrated" — **consequences of a
  Conjectured claim are themselves at most Conjectured.**

- **Refuted** — dead, with the counterexample on the tombstone (F₅ = 641 × 6,700,417), and the
  tombstone *stays visible* so nobody re-runs the autopsy.

- **UNVERIFIED** — said loudly, with the exact wall that blocked verification. An honest "I
  cannot check this yet" outranks a false "it's fine."

## The rules around the labels

- **No silent upgrades. Ever.** Numerics never become Demonstrated by accumulation — twenty
  digits moved Euler to *conjecture* π²/6, not to claim it. Testimony (tickets, READMEs,
  subagent reports, your own memory of last week) never becomes Observed by repetition.
- **Every result states its boundary** — what it does *not* establish ("holds for n ≤ 10⁶; the
  asymptotic regime is dark"; "passes on Linux; the mac path is untested").
- **Evidence strength is graded by Pólya's criteria:** number of verifications; **precision of
  prediction** (a sharp verified prediction outweighs ten loose ones); **rival conjectures** (a
  law with many rivals needs more evidence than an unrivalled one).
- **The moral spine** (Pólya's inductive attitude): "intellectual courage, intellectual
  honesty, and wise restraint are the moral qualities of the scientist." Ready to revise any
  belief; change when there's compelling reason; never change wantonly.

## Why it works — the historical evidence

Euler's two famous wrong conjectures — the 36 officers (E530, 1782) and the sum of powers
(1769) — were both *labeled unproven at birth* ("we have to admit that such an arrangement is
impossible, though we can't give a rigorous demonstration of this"). When they fell (1959–60
and 1966), nothing built on them collapsed, because nothing had been allowed to treat them as
theorems. Meanwhile the pentagonal identity spent a decade at "true but not demonstrated,"
was used constantly under that honest label, and paid out twice: once as a tool, once more
when the proof finally landed (E244, 1750) and everything downstream upgraded in one stroke.

The Ledger is that economy, enforced.
