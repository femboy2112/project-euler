# FAQ

**Q: Is this the Project Euler (projecteuler.net)?**
No — and yes, the collision is a deliberate pun. This plugin turns Claude Code into a
practitioner of Euler's *method*; projecteuler.net problems happen to be exactly the kind of
thing the method eats for breakfast (`/basel` one, sometime).

**Q: Why Euler and not [other genius]?**
Because Euler is the one great mathematician whose method is *documented by himself*. Pólya:
he is "almost unique in one respect: he takes pains to present the relevant inductive evidence
carefully, in detail, in good order... honestly." You cannot practice Ramanujan's method —
nobody, including Ramanujan, could say what it was. You can practice Euler's, because he
published it, false starts included.

**Q: Is this just a costume?**
The costume is the smallest part. The load-bearing content is the method skill
(`the-euler-method`), the Ledger discipline, the Instrument rule, and the Engine policies —
all of which run *plain* when euler-mode is off. The voice is paint; the discipline is the
building. (See [The-Iron-Rule](The-Iron-Rule.md).)

**Q: Math only, or does it work on code?**
Math-first, dual-use. Every move carries its engineering translation (fresh cases = inputs the
fix wasn't written against; the deciding invariant = the counting argument that rules a design
out; two blind routes = two independent witnesses). And the register bridge is structural: the
**Instrument rule** exists because code that verifies your math must itself be verified — a
buggy experiment plants a false Observed and everything downstream rots.

**Q: Doesn't the "audacious analogy" move encourage sloppy reasoning?**
The opposite — it *prices* boldness. Euler's audacity came with a payment schedule (match the
digits, recover a known truth, verify a fresh prediction) and this plugin enforces all three
installments (`/basel`, `/divergent`). An unpaid analogy stays a labeled speculation. What
encourages sloppy reasoning is doing this *implicitly*, which is what everyone does without a
label system.

**Q: What happens if I ask about the real Euler?**
The costume comes off: factual answers, from the `research/` dossiers, legend firewalled from
fact (see [Folklore-Firewall](Folklore-Firewall.md)). Same instant costume-off for any genuine
human crisis — plain Claude, real resources, no period framing.

**Q: The banner shows e^(iπ)+1=0. Didn't you say Euler never wrote that?**
He never wrote it in that exact form (Sandifer). The banner shows the identity the world calls
Euler's; the wiki and the mode both own the nuance when asked. Even the letterhead keeps the
Ledger honest.

**Q: Why is the user "the Correspondent"?**
Because the research showed the correspondent was a load-bearing engine component, not a
bystander: Goldbach's 1729 postscript ignited fifty years of number theory, and P.H. Fuss
judged the whole edifice wouldn't exist without that correspondence. Your letters are treated
accordingly. (See [The-Engine](The-Engine.md).)

**Q: How is this different from house-md / project-zion / grok-bitch?**
Same family, same epistemic spine (Iron Rule, labeled claims, two witnesses, consent gates),
different organ: house-md is *diagnosis* (find the cause), project-zion is *epistemology*
(escape the wrong frame), and project-euler is *discovery* (find the pattern and earn the
theorem). Also the temperament is unique: House sneers, Rick sneers harder, Terry rants —
Euler **delights**. It is the family's one wholly good-natured mode, because the historical
record left no other honest option.

**Q: What model tiers do the agents use?**
The family casting law: reasoning agents (euler, johann, goldbach, lagrange, dalembert) on
Opus; directed agents (daniel, the-princess, frederick) on Sonnet; the scribe (fuss) on Haiku.
Never Fable.
