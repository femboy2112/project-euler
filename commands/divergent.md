# /divergent — *the sum of a series is that quantity which generates the series*

Sometimes the proven road ends and the interesting territory is on the other side of the sign
that says "not valid here." Euler crossed that sign more productively than anyone in history —
summing divergent series, conjecturing the zeta functional equation a century before Riemann —
and the reason it worked is that he crossed it **with a discipline**: redefine deliberately
(never pretend the old rules still apply), and trust a trespassing result only when **routes
that share no machinery agree on it**. Six methods, one value, twelve digits: that is a
license. One method, one value, much hope: that is a wish.

**Parse `$ARGUMENTS`** as the boundary to cross — the method, assumption, or approach to extend
past its proven domain (a formal manipulation, an unproven equivalence, a "what if we just...").
If empty, use the one on the table. If `off`, confirm in one plain sentence and ignore the rest.

## The license

1. **Name the trespass explicitly.** Which assumption is being suspended, which domain boundary
   crossed, what the operation means *now* (Euler didn't mumble about divergent sums — he
   redefined "sum" out loud: the value of the expression that generates the series). A trespass
   nobody names is a bug waiting to be certified.
2. **Open the sandbox.** A scratch directory or throwaway worktree. The real tree is not
   touched; nothing here is committed, merged, or pushed. Everything created inside wears the
   label **FORMAL/SANDBOX** in the report.
3. **Run the extension.** Do the audacious thing, at full technical quality (a sandbox is not an
   excuse for sloppy work — Euler's divergent-series arithmetic was exact).
4. **Pay with multi-route consistency.** Reach the same answer by independent routes — different
   method, different tool, different formulation, sharing as little machinery as possible (a
   second route that reuses the first's core step is one route with an echo). Two agreeing
   routes is a minimum; three is a license. Quote the agreement precisely (digits, cases). Then
   anchor where possible: does the extension **recover known results** inside the proven domain?
   (Euler's functional equation was checked exactly at every integer and numerically at
   half-integers — the trespass was verified everywhere the verified world could see it.)
5. **Rule: GO / NO-GO.** GO — routes agree, known results recovered, and the result (still
   **Conjectured**, always) is worth carrying back as a candidate, with its grounding plan:
   what would Demonstrate it, what would Refute it. NO-GO — routes disagree (report the
   disagreement exactly; it is usually the most informative object in the sandbox) or the
   extension breaks known results. Either way the sandbox is disposable; confirm what was
   discarded.

## Guardrails

- **Nothing escapes unlabeled.** Every artifact of this command is Conjectured-at-best until
  independently grounded; the FORMAL label survives into any downstream use (Euler's
  inheritance rule: consequences of the unproven are themselves unproven).
- **Banned on safety-critical surfaces.** Crypto, production migrations, medical/financial
  logic, anything where a wrong Conjectured is a casualty — the license does not exist there.
- **Consistency is confidence, never proof.** Euler believed all summation methods agree;
  the modern theory says the situation is subtler, and it is (Varadarajan's caveat rides with
  this command). High-precision fraud is real. The Ledger holds.
- Real tree untouched; no commit, no merge, never `git push`.

Close with: the trespass named, the routes and their exact agreement, the known-results anchor,
GO/NO-GO, and — on GO — the grounding plan. The rabbit hole is legal. The label is the rope.
